🎲 My Hobby: Solving Rubik's Cubes

Solving Rubik’s Cubes has been one of my favorite hobbies since high school. I find it satisfying, challenging, and surprisingly calming. It trains both my memory and problem-solving skills.

⚡ My Rubik's Cube Journey

I started with a 3x3 cube during secondary school in 2017 at the age of 14. A friend of mine introduced it to me and taught me how to solve one. Later, I quickly fell in love with it and began to learn more algorithms to solve it faster. After becoming familiar with 3x3, I quickly expanded to 2x2 and 4x4. At my peak, I could solve a standard 3x3 cube in under 25 seconds. But lately, my techniques have been a bit rusty—I haven’t touched a cube in a while, I have forgotten quite a lot of algorithms and my speed has definitely dropped.

Still, it's a skill I’m proud of and one I want to continue practicing. It's not just about speed—it's about patience and logical thinking. When I was younger, I used to think that only a genius could solve a Rubik's Cube. But I was proven wrong. I’m not the most intelligent person, but I was able to solve it. The Rubik's Cube is proof that Nothing is Impossible. If you work hard and don't underestimate yourself, you can accomplish anything.

🎉 Fun Facts About the Cubes

Source: alamy.com

🧮 2x2 Cube

  • Also called a Pocket Cube
  • Only 3,674,160 possible permutations
  • World Record: ~0.47 seconds!

Source: rubiks.com

🧮 3x3 Cube

  • Over 43 quintillion combinations
  • Invented by Erno Rubik in 1974
  • God’s Number is 20: any cube can be solved in 20 moves or fewer

Source: rubiks.com

🧮 4x4 Cube

  • Also known as the Rubik’s Revenge
  • Has no fixed center pieces
  • Parity errors make it trickier than 3x3

📚 How to Solve Rubik’s Cubes

🧰 Solving the 2x2 Cube (Beginner Method)

  • Solve the white face by matching corner pieces.
  • Position yellow corners using R' D' R D moves.
  • Adjust last layer orientation and positioning.

🔄 Solving the 3x3 Cube (CFOP Beginner Path)

  • Step 1: White Cross (Match edge pieces with centers)
  • Step 2: White Corners (Complete white layer)
  • Step 3: Middle Layer Edges
  • Step 4: Yellow Cross → Yellow Corners
  • Step 5: Position & orient yellow corners using PLL & OLL

🔬 Solving the 4x4 Cube (Beginner Method)

  • Step 1: Solve center pieces for all six sides
  • Step 2: Pair edge pieces together
  • Step 3: Solve like a 3x3 cube using CFOP
  • Step 4: Fix parity errors (edge and corner parity)
